The reader
02 / 15

For the sensitive ones.

Sensitive is not the flaw. Sensitive is the information.

The person whose body has always reacted, even to the things that were supposed to help.

The friend who reads every label.

The one who tried the supplement everyone said worked, and felt nothing. Or worse, felt something.

The one who has been let down by a "clean" brand twice and is wary of a third.

She is sensitive in the full sense of the word. Perceptive, not fragile. Twenty-four to forty. Reads what's on the back of the box. Buys fewer things, buys them more deliberately. We built this for her. We are her.

The problem
03 / 15

Half the story.

Most supplements use the right words. Few use the right doses.

Proprietary blends hide the actual amounts. The clinically studied dose is on one slide of the white paper, and a fraction of it is in the bottle. Labels say "blend" because the per-ingredient number would not survive being printed.

For people whose bodies notice everything, this is not a small thing. It is the reason most supplements have failed them. Twice.

Half the story is not the whole story. We chose differently.
Why now
04 / 15

The window is narrow.

Transparency just became a competitive advantage. It will not stay one for long.

Audit-style consumer content has reshaped the category in a single quarter. Premium brands once defended by certifications are being unraveled by side-by-side ingredient comparisons posted from a phone.

Several brands are reaching for the transparency wedge at once. The one who owns sensitive-first, the audience the others are not built for, is the one who will hold the slot.

The wellness shot category was just publicly priced this week. The ingestible-functional comp set has moved. Now is the launch window.

The posture
05 / 15

Notice.
Don't scold.

Care, not correction.

A sensitive person does not raise her voice. She points at the thing. The loudest caption on the feed should still feel like it is in one-on-one conversation. We describe what is already on the label. We do not announce. We cite studies by name. We say clinically studied dose, never "clinically proven." Precision is tone.

When we critique a practice, we never name a competitor. Every critique is a promise we hold ourselves to.

The format
06 / 15

One cap.
Two parts.

A 25 to 30 ml liquid shot. One or two compressed tablets. One unit a day.

The shot
Liquid actives
Bioactive collagen peptides, hyaluronic acid, water-soluble vitamins. The fast-absorbing layer. Pineapple flavored for tolerance, not for marketing.
The tablets
Cofactor delivery
Fat-soluble vitamins and dose-stable compounds in compressed form. The companions that make the shot work. One unit, two parts.

Twenty-eight units per box. Four weeks. The format is the platform. Every product in the line uses the same dual-cap. Same daily ritual. Different function.
